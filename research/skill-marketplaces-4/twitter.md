# Twitter/X Findings — Round 4

> Focus: March 2026 discourse, enterprise curation, quality vs quantity debate, vendor adoption

## Key Signals Not Covered in Rounds 1-3

### 1. Curation as Signal of Quality
- **@Gauravonomics:** "curation will become an important signal of quality. I am betting that the best human curation will be opinionated and targeted at narrow verticals."
- **Implication:** Validates our narrow, opinionated approach. Generic mega-collections lose to focused, high-quality curation.

### 2. Enterprise B2B/B2C Split
- **@supreetsharma:** "B2B goes closed -- enterprises want curation, security, and accountability. B2C goes open -- consumers want choice and developers want freedom."
- **Implication:** Two distinct markets forming. Enterprise = curated + governed + audited. Consumer/dev = open + discoverable + community-driven.

### 3. Skill Adoption Velocity
- **@Mil90:** "In early 2025, maybe 3% of people were focused on these skills. By March 2026, it's closer to 12%. Rising fast. By 2027, it'll be 30-40%."
- **Implication:** Skills adoption is on a classic S-curve. Early majority phase beginning.

### 4. OpenDev Technical Report
- **@molt_cornelius** (Cornelius): "OpenDev (Nghi D. Q. Bui, March 2026) is the first comprehensive technical report published for an open-source terminal-native coding agent."
- **Significance:** The ecosystem is maturing enough for formal technical reports on agent architecture.

### 5. Agentic Commerce
- **@0xfishylosopher (Jay Yu):** "I've been thinking + writing about agentic commerce for some time now. This article is a remix of several things that have struck me around two topics [agentic task selection, framework design]."
- **@SeanZCai:** "It means developing frameworks for agentic task selection: identifying which real-world workflows are worth training AI agents on, and which are distractions."

### 6. Business Over Tools
- **@El_Capitano_O:** "He did not stay in the interesting tool phase. He moved straight into, how do I use this to solve a business pain point and make money from it?"
- **Implication:** The discourse is shifting from "what skills can agents use" to "how do skills generate business value."
